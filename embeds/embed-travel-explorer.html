A natural-sounding fix is to cap how long any single trip can take. Try it: tap Truro and drop the slider to a sensible limit — 6 hours round-trip, say, which is a three-hour drive each way. The map greys out. Push to 8 hours: still only 5 reachable. Truro can't fill a 24-club division until the cap reaches over 10 hours of round-trip driving — or, in miles, around 600 miles round-trip. A hard cap isn't a method of planning a split — it's an impossibility. The geography is what the geography is.
