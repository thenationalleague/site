A natural-sounding fix is to cap how long any single trip can take. Try it: tap a Cornish or north-eastern club and drop the slider to a sensible limit — 6 hours round-trip, say, which is a three-hour drive each way. The map greys out. Try Truro and you'll see they can't even fill a 24-club division within a 10-hour round-trip cap; at 12 hours they still can't reach seven northern clubs. A hard cap isn't a method of planning a split — it's an impossibility. The geography is what the geography is.
