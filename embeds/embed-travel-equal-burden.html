Surely it's unfair that Slough Town drive 60 hours a season and Truro drive 210? Why not equalise every club's burden? The problem is geography: Truro will always travel 210 hours a season, because that's where Cornwall is. The only way to "equalise" is to drag every other club up to match — Brackley's season goes from 109h to 124h, Hebburn pulled into long trips across the country, the map tipped into a bag and shaken. League-wide driving goes up 1,269 hours for the sake of making things "fair." Truro travel exactly as far as they did before. You cannot equalise away geography.
