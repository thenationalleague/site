Ask a computer to find the lowest-driving-time split, with no rules other than 24-and-24, and it sends Truro and Torquay to the North because they're the least costly clubs to add to an already-loose northern group. The maths saves the league 61 hours across the season. The price falls almost entirely on Truro: seven away trips over six hours, including a 7h 26m haul to Hebburn. Their season jumps by 40 hours so the rest of the league can save 61 between them. That isn't equitable in any meaningful sense.
