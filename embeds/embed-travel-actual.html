The answer: a 24/24 split that puts Merthyr in the North (the only viable Welsh-and-west group) and keeps Truro, Torquay, Weston and Salisbury in the South. League-wide it costs 4,719 hours of round-trip driving across 1,104 fixtures — just 1.3% above the lowest number a computer can produce, and dramatically better than every other rule-of-thumb method. Brackley, Bedford and Oxford absorb the friction of being central; Truro keeps the long trips at a manageable maximum. Imperfect, but the price of having a league.
